# Day 1: Understanding Social Anxiety & Intro to CBT

## Lesson Content

Social anxiety is characterized by intense fear of social situations and being judged by others. Today we explore the physical symptoms, including racing heart, sweating, and stomach discomfort. Remember that these reactions are your nervous system's fight-or-flight response misinterpreting social situations as threats.

In social anxiety, your nervous system interprets social situations as threats. This is not your fault - it's a learned response that can be unlearned. Today's lesson will help you start this process by becoming aware of your body's signals.

But here's the thing: Social anxiety isn't some unfixable thing about you. It's more like a bad habit your brain picked up. And just like any habit, you can change it. That's where CBT comes in—Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. Sounds fancy, but it's basically about noticing the stuff your brain tells you, deciding if it's actually true, and then flipping the script.

Here's how it works: Your thoughts, feelings, and actions are all connected, like a little anxiety triangle. Let's say you're at a party, and you think, 'Everyone's staring at me because I look awkward.' That thought makes you feel anxious, which might make you hide in the corner or leave early. But here's the thing—most of the time, those thoughts aren't even true. CBT helps you catch those thoughts, challenge them, and reframe them into something more real.

## Journal Prompts

- Describe a recent situation where you felt socially anxious. What physical sensations did you notice? Where in your body did you feel them most strongly?
- What thoughts typically go through your mind during anxious moments?
- In your own words, what is social anxiety? Write it out like you'd explain it to a friend.
- Why do you think starting with awareness matters?

## Daily Exercise

Practice the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique when feeling anxious:
- Acknowledge 5 things you can see
- 4 things you can touch
- 3 things you can hear
- 2 things you can smell
- 1 thing you can taste

Your first challenge is to figure out where your social anxiety shows up the most. Write down three situations where you feel it hit the hardest, and think about why those moments feel so intense. Is it fear of being judged? Worrying you'll say the wrong thing? Whatever it is, jot it down.

## Motivation

Recognizing your anxiety patterns is the first step to overcoming them. By simply completing today's exercises, you've already begun your journey toward freedom from social anxiety.

You're already doing something awesome just by being here. Start small and don't overthink it—this is about getting curious, not judging yourself.
