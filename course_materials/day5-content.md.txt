# Day 5: Take a Small Step – Facing Your Fears Gradually

## Lesson Content

You've made it to Day 5, and today, we're taking action. You've built your fear ladder—now it's time to take the first step. Don't worry—it's not about conquering your biggest fear all at once. It's about starting small and proving to yourself that you can handle it.

Think of it like dipping your toes into cold water before jumping in. Each small step builds your confidence, and before you know it, the big fears won't feel so big anymore. Today, we're focusing on that first step. It might feel uncomfortable, and that's okay—discomfort means you're growing.

Let's talk about handling the anxiety that shows up when you take that step. Anxiety is like a wave—it starts strong but fades if you ride it out. Here are some quick strategies to help before and during the moment.

First, before you face your fear, try these:
- Deep breaths: In through your nose for four seconds, out through your mouth for six
- Positive self-talk: Remind yourself, 'This is just a small step—I've got this'
- Visualize success: Picture yourself completing the step and feeling proud

Now, during the moment, keep these in mind:
- Stay present: Focus on what you see, hear, or feel to ground yourself
- Pause if needed: It's okay to take a moment to breathe and reset
- Celebrate afterward: No matter how it goes, showing up is a win!

Every small step you take teaches your brain that you can face your fears. It's like leveling up in a game—you're earning confidence with every move. Your mission today: Take that first step on your fear ladder. It doesn't have to be perfect—progress is what counts.

## Journal Prompts

- What's the first step on your fear ladder that you'll attempt today?
- What coping strategies will you use before and during this step?
- How will you celebrate taking this step, regardless of the outcome?

## Daily Exercise

Complete the "Pre-Step Planning Worksheet":
- Write down the fear ladder step you'll take
- Check off your pre-step strategies (deep breaths, positive self-talk, visualization)
- Note how you feel before attempting the step (emoji scale)

After attempting your step, fill out the "Post-Step Reflection Template":
- Describe what you did
- Note what happened
- Check how you felt afterward (emoji scale)
- Write what went well and what you might try next time
- Share one thing you're proud of from the experience
