# Day 7: Assert Yourself – The Power of Boundaries

## Lesson Content

Welcome to Day 7 of Social Anxiety Reset! Today, we're diving into a skill that's a total game-changer: assertiveness. You've probably heard the term, but what does it really mean? Let's break it down and learn how to set boundaries that work for you—without guilt.

Assertiveness is all about balancing respect—respect for yourself and for others. Here's how it compares:
- Passiveness: You prioritize others' needs over your own, often saying 'yes' when you mean 'no.'
- Aggressiveness: You prioritize your needs but dismiss others, often coming across as rude or harsh.
- Assertiveness: The sweet spot—you express your needs clearly and respectfully, finding a win-win whenever possible.

Let's be real: For a lot of us, especially Gen Z, setting boundaries can feel awkward. We worry about hurting others or being labeled as selfish. But here's the truth: Boundaries aren't about shutting people out—they're about making space for yourself. And when you don't set boundaries, it can lead to burnout, resentment, and—you guessed it—anxiety.

Boundaries are like the rules in a video game—they tell others what's okay and what's not. Without them, you might find yourself constantly drained or saying yes to things that don't feel right. When you set a boundary, you're saying, 'I value myself and my needs.' And here's the best part: People who care about you will respect that.

I know what you're thinking:
- 'What if they think I'm mean?'
- 'What if they stop talking to me?'

Here's the thing: True friends and supportive people will respect your honesty. And for those who don't? That says more about them than you.

See how assertiveness works? It's about owning your needs without guilt or apology.

## Daily Exercise

Complete the "Understanding Assertiveness Worksheet":
- Label different responses as passive, aggressive, or assertive
- Example: "Your friend borrows your sweater and ruins it. You say:
  - 'It's fine, don't worry about it.' (Passive)
  - 'You ruined my sweater! How could you?' (Aggressive)
  - 'I'm upset that the sweater got ruined. Can we talk about replacing it?' (Assertive)"
- Reflect on which style you tend to use most often and why

Practice with the "Assertiveness Script Practice" worksheet:
- Use sample scripts for saying no, expressing a need, and handling criticism
- Fill in the blanks: "When __ happens, I feel __. I'd like __ instead."
- Example: "When people interrupt me, I feel frustrated. I'd like to finish my point before others jump in."

Setting boundaries takes courage, and you're doing great just by thinking about it!

Every time you practice assertiveness, you're building stronger, healthier relationships.
